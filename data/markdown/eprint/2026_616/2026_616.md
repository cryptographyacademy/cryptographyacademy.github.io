#### Abstract

Arithmetic correlation is a critical performance measure for pseudorandom sequences generated by feedback with carry shift registers (FCSRs), extending classical correlation by accounting for carry propagation. Chen et al. proved that for binary sequences with coprime periods, the arithmetic crosscorrelation is constant, and established bounds for Legendre sequences and m-sequences. In this paper, we further investigate the arithmetic crosscorrelation of sequences with coprime periods. We derive upper bounds for sequences constructed from the Legendre symbol, which generalize classical Legendre sequences, and for sequences generated by trace functions. In addition, we show that the constant property of arithmetic crosscorrelation extends to non-binary sequences with coprime periods.

Keywords: Pseudorandom sequence, Arithmetic crosscorrelations, Legendre symbol, Trace sequences

# <span id="page-0-0"></span>1 Introduction

Pseudorandom sequences are generated by shift registers and deterministic algorithms, and play a vital role in fields such as communications, radar, and stream ciphers [\[1,](#page-9-0) [2\]](#page-9-1). In the 1990s, Klapper and Goresky introduced the feedback with carry shift register 

{1}------------------------------------------------

(FCSR) sequence, which can be viewed as an arithmetic analog of the linear feedback shift register (LFSR) sequence [3].

One important measure for FCSR sequences is arithmetic correlation. The concept of arithmetic autocorrelation was introduced by Mandelbaum [4] in his research on arithmetic codes, and was later extended to arithmetic correlation by Goresky and Klapper in their study of FCSR sequences [2, 5].

For two binary sequences  $S = (s_i)_{i=0}^{N-1}$  and  $T = (t_i)_{i=0}^{N-1}$  with a common minimal period N, where  $s_i, t_i \in \{0, 1\}$ , their arithmetic crosscorrelation can be defined as follows, according to [2, Proposition 13.3.8] and [6]:

Write

$$S(2) = \sum_{i=0}^{N-1} s_i 2^i, \ T^{(\tau)}(2) = \sum_{i=0}^{N-1} t_{i+\tau} 2^i.$$

The integer  $|S(2) - T^{(\tau)}(2)| \ge 0$  can be expressed in 2-adic expansion  $|S(2) - T^{(\tau)}(2)| = \sum_{i=0}^{N-1} w_i 2^i (w_i \in \{0,1\})$ . The arithmetic crosscorrelation of S and T at a shift  $\tau$  is defined as

$$C_{S,T}^{A}(\tau) = \begin{cases} N_0 - N_1, & \text{if } S(2) \ge T^{(\tau)}(2), \\ N_1 - N_0, & \text{if } S(2) < T^{(\tau)}(2), \end{cases}$$
(1)

where  $N_j$  is the number of i = 0, 1, ..., N - 1 with  $w_i = j$  for each  $j \in \{0, 1\}$ .

Recently, Hofer, Merai, and Winterhof [7] established a relationship between arithmetic autocorrelation and correlation measure of order k, showing that binary sequences with sufficiently small higher order correlation measures cannot exhibit large arithmetic autocorrelation, and applies this result to several families of sequences, including Legendre sequences defined by polynomials and trace function sequences over finite fields. For sequences with coprime periods, Chen et al. [8] proved that their arithmetic crosscorrelation is constant and provided an upper bound for the arithmetic crosscorrelation between Legendre sequences and m-sequences with such periods. Jing et al. [9] later improved the results for the arithmetic crosscorrelation of m-sequences with coprime periods, obtaining the exact value. Related work by Yan et al. [10] established an upper bound estimate for the arithmetic crosscorrelation of Fermat quotient related sequences with coprime periods.

Inspired by the above studies, in Sections 2 and 3 of this paper, we investigate the arithmetic crosscorrelation of sequences constructed from the Legendre symbol and the trace function with coprime periods, respectively, and obtain upper bounds for them. Here, sequences constructed from the Legendre symbol can be regarded as generalizations of Legendre sequences. In Section 4, we prove that the arithmetic crosscorrelation of non-binary sequences with coprime periods is also a constant.

In this paper, we say that h(n) = O(l(n)) (also written as  $h(n) \ll l(n)$ ) if there exists an absolute constant C > 0 such that  $|h(n)| \leq Cl(n)$  for all n. For sequence  $S = (s_i)_{i \geq 0}$  with period N, define  $s_{-i} = s_{N-i}$ . And log denotes the natural logarithm.

# <span id="page-7-0"></span>4 Arithmetic crosscorrelation of non-binary sequences of coprime period

While Theorem 1 of |8| establishes the constancy of the arithmetic crosscorrelation for the binary coprime period case, the result in fact extends to non-binary sequences as well.

Let  $S = (s_i)_{i\geq 0}$  and  $T = (t_i)_{i\geq 0}$  be two periodic sequences over  $\mathbb{Z}_m$  $\{0, 1, \dots, m-1\}$  with periods p and q, respectively, and  $\gcd(p, q) = 1$ . Denote N = pq, the common period of S and T. Let  $\zeta_m = e^{2\pi\sqrt{-1}/m}$ . For a fixed integer  $\tau$ , define the arithmetic crosscorrelation  $C_{\mathcal{S},\mathcal{T}}^A(\tau)$  of  $\mathcal{S}$  and  $\mathcal{T}$  as follows. Represent

$$\alpha = \sum_{i=0}^{\infty} s_i m^i, \qquad \beta_{\tau} = \sum_{i=0}^{\infty} t_{i+\tau} m^i,$$

and let  $\alpha - \beta_{\tau} = \sum_{i=0}^{\infty} u_i m^i$  be the (eventually periodic) m-adic expansion. Let j be the length of the preperiod. Then

$$C_{\mathcal{S},\mathcal{T}}^{A}(\tau) = \sum_{i=j}^{j+N-1} \zeta_m^{u_i} = \sum_{j=0}^{m-1} M_j \zeta_m^j,$$
 (4)

where  $M_j$  counts the times j appears in one full period of the m-adic expansion of  $\alpha - \beta_{\tau}$ .

<span id="page-7-1"></span>**Theorem 3** Let  $m \geq 2$  be an integer. Let S and T be two periodic sequences over  $\mathbb{Z}_m$ with coprime periods p and q, respectively. Then the arithmetic crosscorrelation  $C_{\mathcal{S},\mathcal{T}}^A(\tau)$  is independent of  $\tau$ .

*Proof* Write N = pq. We define the first N coefficients of the periodic part of the difference sequence between sequence  $\mathcal S$  and sequence  $\mathcal T$  as

$$\Delta_{\tau} = \sum_{i=0}^{N-1} \omega_i m^i.$$

for  $\omega_i \in \{0, 1, \dots, m-1\}$ .

Let  $S(m) = \sum_{i=0}^{N-1} s_i m^i$  and  $T^{(\tau)}(m) = \sum_{i=0}^{N-1} t_{i+\tau} m^i$ , according the Proposition 13.3.8 in [2] and an analysis analogous to that in [6], one can check that either

$$S(m) - T^{(\tau)}(m) = \Delta_{\tau}$$

with  $S(m) \ge T^{(\tau)}(m)$ , otherwise

$$m^{N} - 1 + (S(m) - T^{(\tau)}(m)) = \Delta_{\tau}.$$

{8}------------------------------------------------

Hence the N-tuple  $(u_j, \ldots, u_{j+N-1})$  is uniquely determined by  $\Delta_{\tau}$  itself (as an integer between 0 and  $m^N - 2$ ).

Now fix  $\tau$  and let x be the unique integer  $0 \le x < q$  such that

$$x \equiv \tau p^{-1} \pmod{q},$$

which exists because gcd(p,q) = 1. Set

$$\lambda_k = \sum_{i=0}^{p-1} (s_i - t_{i+kp}) m^i, \qquad k = 0, 1, \dots, q-1.$$

Because  $\mathcal{T}$  has period q, we have  $\lambda_{k+q} = \lambda_k$ . Then

$$m^{xp}\Delta_{\tau} = \sum_{n=0}^{N-1} (s_n - t_{n+\tau}) m^{n+xp}.$$

Write  $n = n_1 + n_2 p$  with  $0 \le n_1 < p$ ,  $0 \le n_2 < q$ . Let  $n_3 \equiv x + n_2 \pmod{q}$  with  $0 \le n_3 < q$ . Using the periodicity of  $\mathcal{S}$  and  $\mathcal{T}$ , we obtain

$$m^{xp}\Delta_{\tau} \equiv \sum_{n_3=0}^{q-1} \lambda_{n_3} m^{n_3 p} \pmod{m^N - 1}.$$
 (5)

Denote

$$\Omega = \sum_{n_3=0}^{q-1} \lambda_{n_3} m^{n_3 p} \pmod{m^N - 1},$$

notice that  $\Omega$  does not depend on  $\tau$ .

Now consider the m-adic expansion sequences. Multiplication by  $m^{xp}$  modulo  $m^N - 1$  corresponds to a cyclic right shift of the N coefficients m-adic representation by xp positions. This shift does not alter the multiset of digits in one full period. Eq. (5) therefore implies that the sequence corresponding to  $\Delta_{\tau}$  is a cyclic shift of the sequence corresponding to  $\Omega$ . Consequently, for every  $\tau$ , the number of occurrences of each  $j \in \mathbb{Z}_m$  in the N coefficients period of  $\Delta_{\tau}$  is exactly the same as in  $\Omega$ .

#### 5 Conclusions

In this paper, we studied the arithmetic crosscorrelation of sequences with coprime periods. For sequences constructed from the Legendre symbol, which generalize the classical Legendre sequences, we established an upper bound of  $O(dp^{1/2}r(\log p)^2)$  for distinct prime periods p < r. For sequences generated by trace functions, we obtained a bound of  $O(\max(dp^{\frac{1}{2}}r\log p, d^2pr^{\frac{1}{2}}))$  for periods  $p = 2^{n_1} - 1$  and  $r = 2^{n_2} - 1$  with  $\gcd(n_1, n_2) = 1$ .

Furthermore, we extended the constant property of arithmetic crosscorrelation to the non-binary case, proving that for any two sequences with coprime periods, the arithmetic crosscorrelation is independent of the shift.

Future work may explore the arithmetic crosscorrelation for other families of sequences with coprime periods, such as non-binary Legendre sequences and non-binary m-sequences.
